Remarks at USS Arkansas Propulsion Sea Trial

1980 Speech Source: USNA Archives Box 27
View Original PDF View Original TXT

Summary

This document contains Admiral Hyman G. Rickover's comments from September 1, 1980, celebrating the successful conclusion of the USS Arkansas's first propulsion sea trial, which included a 4-hour full power run exceeding 30 knots. Rickover commends the Tenneco management and the "ready made and fine organization" of designers, engineers, and trades people at Newport News, citing the yard's historic motto, "We will build good ships at a profit if we can, at a loss if we must, but always good ships," established by its founder, C.P. Huntington. He particularly praises the officers and men of the *Arkansas*, noting with admiration that an impressive 60% of the crew had no prior sea experience, yet they "handled the ship like veterans" and demonstrated outstanding capability.

1 SEPTEMBER 1980
ADMIRAL RICKOVER‘s COMMENTS AT THE CONCLUSION OF THE ARKANSAS
PROPULSION SEA TRIAL:

1, WE HAVE JUST COMPLETED THE FIRST SEA TRIAL OF THE ARKANSAS,

THE PROPULSION PLANT SUCCESSFULLY PASSED THE TRIALS, INCLUDING

A 4-HOUR FULL POWER RUN AT A SPEED GREATER THAN 30 KNOTS.

2, THE TENNECO MANAGEMENT SHOULD BE GRATEFUL THAT IT TOOK

OVER SUCH A READY MADE AND FINE ORGANIZATION OF DESIGNERS,
ENGINEERS, DRAFTMEN, AND TRADES PEOPLE. CHET LONG WHO HAS BEEN

IN CHARGE OF THE TRIALS FOR NEWPORT NEWS, IS AN EXAMPLE OF MANY
OTHERS WHO HAVE FOR YEARS DESIGNED AND BUILT GOOD SHIPS, THE
MOTTO OF NEWPORT NEWS USED TO BE “WE WILL BUILD Goon SHIPS AT

A PROFIT IF WE CAN, AT A LOSS IF WE MUST, BUT ALWAYS GOOD SHIPS,”
THIS MOTTO WAS SET BY MR. C.P, HUNTINGTON, THE RAILROAD MAN WHO
FOUNDED THE YARD IN THE 1880's. IT USED TO BE DISPLAYED ON A |
ROCK IN THE SHIPYARD; IT IS NOW IN THE MUSEUM,

3. I WANT TO CONGRATULATE ALL OF THE SKILLED PEOPLE WHO BUILT THIS
SHIP. I ALSO WANT TO THANK THE SUPERVISOR OF SHIPBUILDING, THE
REPRESENTATIVES OF THE BOARD OF INSPECTION AND SURVEY, THE

NAVY AND THE CONTRACTOR REPRESENTATIVE, ALL WORKED HARD TO BRING
ABOUT THIS SUCCESSFUL TRIAL.

4, BUT PARTICULARLY I WANT TO CONGRATULATE THE OFFICERS AND

MEN OF THE ARKANSAS. ANYONE WITNESSING THESE TRIALS WOULD FIND

IT HARD TO BELIEVE THAT 60% OF THE CREW HAVE HAD No PREVIOUS SEA
EXPERIENCE,

5. THE MEN AND OFFICERS HAVE DEMONSTRATED THEY ARE CAPABLE OF
DOING AN OUTSTANDING JOB, YOU HANDLED THE SHIP LIKE VETERANS,

I KNOW YOU WILL CONTINUE TO PERFORM YOUR DUTIES IN THE SAME
OUTSTANDING MANNER. OUR COUNTRY IS PROUD OF CAPTAIN REED,
HIS OFFICERS, AND HIS MEN,

6. IN BEHALF OF THE NAVY I THANK YOU FOR A JOB WELL DONE,
