Remarks at USS San Francisco Propulsion Sea Trial

1981 Speech Source: USNA Archives Box 27
View Original PDF View Original TXT

Summary

Delivered at sea in the North Atlantic on February 9, 1981, Admiral Rickover's remarks commemorate the successful initial sea trials of the USS *San Francisco*, commending the engineers and craftsmen of Newport News for their excellent work and praising Commander Marshall and his crew for their outstanding performance, despite many being new to sea duty. Beyond the technical report, Rickover delivers a "homily," reflecting on the vast changes from his early 20th-century childhood—a time of horses and darkness—to the modern era of nuclear weapons, space travel, and the silicon chip. He laments a perceived societal shift, noting the absence of a "unifying faith" and a decline in reliance on reason and common sense. Rickover passionately advocates for a rebirth of honorable conduct, confidence in human reason, and national pride, asserting these non-material ideals are fundamental to a nation's true greatness.

T SEA
ORTH ATLANTIC
9 Fepruary 1981

ADMIRAL RICKOVER’S REMARKS ON THE
SAN FRANCISCO SEA TRIALS

WE HAVE JUST COMPLETED THE FIRST SEA TRIALS OF THE SAN
FRANCISCO. THE PROPULSION PLANT OPERATED SATISFACTORILY,

I WANT ONCE AGAIN TO CONGRATULATE THE ENGINEERS AND CRAFTSMEN
OF NEWPORT NEWS FOR THEIR EXCELLENT WORK, EVIDENCED BY THESE
SUCCESSFUL TRIALS. THE MEN AND WOMEN OF NEWPORT NEWS CONTINUE
THE TRADITION OF SHIPBUILDING COMPETENCE THE NAVY COUNTS ON.

] ESPECIALLY WANT TO CONGRATULATE THE OFFICERS AND MEN OF
THE SAN FRANCISCO. ANYONE WITNESSING THESE TRIALS WOULD FIND IT
HARD TO BELIEVE THAT ONLY HALF THE CREW HAS BEEN TO SEA BEFORE
THESE TRIALS EXCEPT FOR SHORT TRAINING PERIODS.

THE MEN AND OFFICERS OF THE SHIP HAVE DEMONSTRATED THEY ARE
CAPABLE OF DOING AN OUTSTANDING JOB. I KNOW YOU WILL CONTINE
TO PERFORM YOUR DUTIES IN THIS SAME OUTSTANDING MANNER. OUR
COUNTRY SHOULD BE PROUD OF COMMANDER MARSHALL, HIS OFFICERS, AND
HIS MEN,

IN BEHALF OF THE NAVY I THANK ALL OF YOU FOR A JOB WELL DONE.

SINCE NO CHURCH SERVICES COULD BE HELD IN THE SAN FRANCISCO
THIS SUNDAY, I AM TAKING ADVANTAGE OF THIS OCCASION TO DELIVER
A LITTLE HOMILY:

I WAS BORN IN THE FIRST YEAR OF THIS CENTURY AND EXPERIENCED
A CHILDHOOOD WHICH HAD NOT CHANGED MUCH SINCE THAT OF THE TIME OF
THE CIVIL WAR. I NOW LIVE IN THE AGE OF THE NUCLEAR WEAPON,
SPACE TRAVEL, AND THE SILICON CHIP. THESE DIVERSE EXPERIENCES
MAKE ME FEEL AS THOUGH I HAVE BEEN A PARTICIPANT IN TWO SEPARATE
LIVES. BUT I AM STILL MUCH THE SAME PERSON I WAS IN THOSE EARLY

ons: THERE WERE THEN NO AUTOMOBILES ON THE ROAD; HORSES WERE
STILL ‘MAN’S MOST IMPORTANT COMPANION AND FRIEND FROM THE ANIMAL
WORLD. AT NIGHT WITH*NO ELECTRICITY, THERE WAS TOTAL DARKNESS).
ONLY LIT BY THE MOON OR STARS, EXCEPT FOR THE BARKING OF A DOG
OR THE HOOF-BEATS OF A HORSE, IT WAS QUIET,
_ IN THE PRESENT AGE THERE 1S NO UNIFYING FAITH 79 BIND US
TOGETHER: IN ITS PLACE THERE 1S ONLY A HALE-HEARTED ADHERENCE
TO MANY CONFLICTING AND DIVISIVE FORMS OF IDEOLSGICAL OR
EMOTIONAL NONSENSE,

ANOTHER ATTRIBUTE OF THAT VANISHED AGE WHICH DISTINGUISHED
IT FROM THAT OF THE PRESENT DAY WAS A RELIANCE OF REASONS ON THE
CUMULATIVE KNOWLEDGE OF THE PASTs ON COMMON SENSE, INSTEAD OF
“Pop”, OR FASHION OR CRAZE,

DESPITE THE VAST IMPROVEMENT IN LIVING AND SOCIAL CONDITIONS
AND THE GREAT TECHNICAL AND SCIENTIFIC ADVANCES THAT 1 HAVE
WITNESSED IN MY LIFETIME, THERE IS ONE THING I SHOULD LIKE TO SEE
REBORN WHICH IS MISSING IN THE MATERIALLY GUIDED UNITED, STATES

AND DEALINGS; A CONFIDENCE IN MEN AND WOMEN OF ALL CLASSES AND

CALLINGS IN THEIR OWN CAPACITY FOR REASON AND GOOD SENSE; AND

A PRIDE IN OUR COUNTRY AND ITS ACHIEVEMENTS, PAST, PRESENT AND

FUTURE. .
THIS WAS FORMERLY SHARED BY ARISTOCRAT, BOURGEOIS AND WORKING

PEOPLE ALIKE, THESE, DESPITE ALL THE DIFFERENCES IN THEIR

INDIVIDUAL CIRCUMSTANCES AND WAYS OF LIFE, MADE US UNITED AS

ONE NATION, AND THESE IDEALS, WHICH CANNOT BE SERVED BY MONEY

ALONE, ARE WHAT MAKE A PEOPLE GREAT. AND WHAT OUR PEOPLE ARE ~

1S WHAT OUR COUNTRY IS,
