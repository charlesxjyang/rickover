Summary

This document, titled "The Computer and Invasion of Privacy," offers a compelling look into critical discussions that took place during the 89th Congress, spanning July 1966 to January 1967. It explores the early emergence of widespread concern over the potential implications of computer technology for individual privacy, marking a foundational period when the legislative branch began to grapple with issues of data security and personal information rights. The title highlights a prescient debate from a time when the societal impact of computing was still in its infancy, setting the stage for ongoing discussions about technology, ethics, and governmental oversight.
